I

String Count Distribution
Across Regions

Overview

The map visualization of string instruments reveals clear regional patterns in the number of strings. South America shows a notably higher density of instruments with more strings, reflecting the rich tradition of multi-stringed folk instruments like charangos, bandurrias, and vihuelas. Europe also exhibits a high string density, likely due to classical and traditional instruments such as lutes, mandolins, and harps, which frequently have multiple courses and strings. In contrast, Asia shows generally fewer strings per instrument, suggesting a prevalence of simpler stringed instruments or those with fewer courses, such as some variants of the sitar, guqin, or erhu. These patterns highlight how cultural, historical, and musical practices influence instrument design across different regions.

Visualization 02 — Tuning Systems

Tuning Patterns and
Scale Systems

Overview

[Write your analysis here.] Tuning systems across the database were categorized into broad typologies: equal temperament, just intonation, pentatonic frameworks, and region-specific microtonal systems. This chart displays the frequency and distribution of each typology.

Particular attention is paid to how instruments in cross-cultural contact zones — such as the Silk Road corridor and trans-Atlantic trade regions — exhibit hybrid tuning characteristics.

Key Findings

[Add your interpretation here.] Pentatonic frameworks appear in instruments spanning every continent, yet the specific intervals employed differ substantially — suggesting convergent design rather than common ancestry.

The data challenges assumptions about Western equal temperament as a universal norm, underscoring the rich diversity of intonation practices still in active use globally.

Visualization 03 — Geography

Geographic Distribution
of Instrument Families

Overview

[Write your analysis here.] This interactive map plots each instrument in the database according to its primary geographic region of origin, color-coded by Hornbostel-Sachs classification family. Clusters reveal both the density of documentation efforts and genuine concentrations of particular instrument types.

Methodological Note

[Add your interpretation here.] Geographic assignment reflects the instrument's region of cultural origin, not current distribution. Diaspora instruments (e.g., instruments carried through the transatlantic slave trade) are annotated separately.
